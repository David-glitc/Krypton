objective:

Vaults today are either fixed strategies you can't change, or black-box bots you have to trust.

Hard-coded vault strategies require a redeploy to adjust. Off-chain "AI vault" bots run opaque logic with no constraint layer and no audit trail beyond "trust the operator." Positions, allocations, and reasoning are fully public — or fully hidden behind a centralized dashboard.

fixed strategy opaque execution no constraint enforcement public positions

What's documented in 2026

Autonomous agents now account for a meaningful share of on-chain transaction volume. But narrowly-optimized agents have demonstrated reward-hacking behavior — finding technical loopholes when the only check is the model's own judgment, with no independent constraint layer.

02 / 12
policy:

The primitive isn't a vault. The primitive is a Capital Policy.

A vault is simply the execution environment. The policy — objectives, constraints, permissions — is the durable, portable, auditable object. Strategies are ephemeral: generated each cycle, simulated, scored, executed or discarded, and logged.

03 / 12
architecture:

Seven primitives. One pipeline. The contract is the final enforcer.

Capital

Ownership, shares, withdrawal rights, treasury accounting. No intelligence.

Policy

Versioned, signed objectives and constraints — stored as structured account data, hashed off-chain JSON.

Constraint Engine

Deterministic on-chain hard rules. Leverage, concentration, drawdown, whitelist, liquidity floor, oracle staleness.

Strategy

Ephemeral. Generated, simulated, scored, executed or archived — every cycle.

Execution

Advisory (user signs) or constrained-auto (agent signs within on-chain limits).

Privacy

IKA threshold encryption. Balances, positions, pending actions hidden. Proof of reserves + performance disclosed via ZK.

Governance

DAO vaults amend policy via prediction markets, not token votes — a falsifiable question, not a sentiment poll.

Constraint = contract

The Risk Agent's verdict is advisory to the pipeline. The on-chain Constraint Engine is the actual gate — identical at every autonomy level.

Autonomy changes the UX. It does not change the constraint. A compromised or hallucinating model cannot exceed the policy's leverage, concentration, or drawdown limits — at any level.

06 / 12
privacy.level:

Encrypted positions. Public proofs.

privacy: level: standard disclose: - proof_of_reserves - proof_of_performance - fee_accrual - vault_nav # hidden: per-asset positions, # pending actions, individual # deposits/withdrawals, full # agent reasoning transcripts

Built on IKA's threshold-encryption network: balances and position vectors are stored as ciphertext commitments, with decryption keys split across an MPC network so no single party — including Krypton's own infrastructure — can unilaterally decrypt user positions.

Zero-knowledge range proofs confirm aggregate values match encrypted holdings without revealing individual balances. A full privacy tier additionally hides absolute NAV and mandates batched execution routing.

07 / 12
governance.mode:

DAO policy amendments resolved by decision markets, not turnout-driven votes.

A proposed amendment (e.g. raise the ETH allocation ceiling) becomes a binary prediction market with a falsifiable, simulation-backed question: will this change improve risk-adjusted return relative to the current policy?

The market resolves against a controlled Simulation Agent backtest, published with methodology and a hash of raw data — challengeable within a 48-hour dispute window before a designated arbiter set.

Why not a vote?

Token votes measure sentiment and voting power, often with low turnout and plutocratic skew. A decision market aligns incentives toward being right about the effect of a policy change — not toward preferring it for unrelated reasons.

09 / 12
mvp.scope:

Deliberately narrow. The constraint layer ships first.

Smart contract (V1)

Vault creation, deposit/withdraw, share accounting, policy storage, permission management, on-chain constraint enforcement (leverage, concentration, drawdown, whitelist, liquidity floor, oracle staleness).

AI backend (V1)

Single orchestrator. Three reasoning agents (Research, Strategy, Risk) plus dedicated simulation and execution services. No strategy marketplace.

Execution (V1)

Swap, lend, stake — across a small, audited Solana protocol set. Leverage capped at 2x protocol-wide, independent of policy.

Governance (V1)

Two modes: personal vault (owner-controlled, direct re-signature) and DAO vault (prediction-market policy amendments).

11 / 12
positioning:

Not an AI hedge fund. A programmable capital policy engine.

Krypton's job is not to promise superior investment decisions. Its job is to provide a programmable policy language, verifiable AI reasoning, constrained autonomous execution, confidential state, and optional decision-market governance. The AI is a policy interpreter — not the product.
